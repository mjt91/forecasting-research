## [2606.06190] Multi-Scale Markov Switching GARCH
**Why relevant:** Direct multi-timescale volatility regime detection for EUR/USD; TVTP-MS-GARCH produces superior out-of-sample vol forecasts vs GARCH benchmark
**Key takeaways:**
- Multi-timescale (daily/4H/1H) MS-GARCH captures macro/meso/micro regime structure that single-timescale misses
- 27-state cross-scale tensor constructed via outer product of per-scale regime probabilities
- Filardo-style TVTP lets regime transition probabilities respond to market stress indicators
- Statistically distinct Calm/Turbulent/Crisis regimes identified in EUR/USD 2015-2025
**Technique:** Triple AR(1)-MS-GARCH with TVTP, outer-product probability tensor

## [2606.05138] SOCK: Random Convolutional Features for Financial Time Series
**Why relevant:** Addresses small-sample overfitting in synthetic financial TS generation; differentiable alternative to ROCKET/Hydra
**Key takeaways:**
- SOCK (SOft Competing Kernels) makes random convolutional features differentiable — enables adversarial training of generators
- Outperforms signature-based and diffusion baselines on small-sample financial datasets
- Also applicable to two-sample hypothesis testing and TS classification
- Relevant to [[sbbts-schrodinger-bass-synthetic-ts]] for data augmentation in production forecasting
**Technique:** Differentiable random convolutional feature matching, generative adversarial training

## [2606.04153] Sign-Magnitude Return Decomposition
**Why relevant:** Captures nonlinear return predictability via sign-magnitude conditioning; competitive with copula methods on monthly US stock returns
**Key takeaways:**
- Decomposing returns R = sign × |R| separates volatility modeling (magnitude) from directional prediction (sign)
- Conditional sign distribution given magnitude captures asymmetric return dynamics
- Outperforms linear regression and complete subset regression; competitive with copula benchmarks
- Journal of Banking and Finance publication (accepted manuscript)
**Technique:** Sign-magnitude decomposition, conditional distribution modeling
