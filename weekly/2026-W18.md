## [2604.27696] FoReco and FoRecoML: A Unified Toolbox for Forecast Reconciliation in R

**Why relevant:** Directly addresses hierarchical/top-down reconciliation — underused technique in finance production forecasting systems. The first unified R toolbox covering cross-sectional, temporal, AND cross-temporal reconciliation with both linear and ML-based approaches.

**Key takeaways:**
1. Forecast reconciliation optimally combines hierarchical/grouped time series forecasts while respecting linear constraints — improves accuracy at aggregated levels where most decisions happen
2. FoRecoML's ML-based (XGBoost/LightGBM) reconciliation captures nonlinear relationships between hierarchy levels that linear MinT-style approaches miss
3. Cross-temporal reconciliation (simultaneous spatial + temporal aggregation) fills a gap not covered by Nixtla's `hierarchicalforecast` Python library

**Technique:** Classical linear reconciliation (trace minimization) + regression/ML-based reconciliation for cross-sectional, temporal, and cross-temporal hierarchies. R packages on CRAN.

**Production implication:** If you're building hierarchical forecasts (product/category/division; region/national), reconciliation is a cheap post-hoc step that consistently improves aggregated-level accuracy. Test against your current bottom-up or top-down approach.

## [2604.22801] Context-Integrated Adversarial Learning for Stock Price Prediction

**Why relevant:** Addresses the regime-switching problem — models trained in calm markets fail during volatility spikes — using adversarial (GAN-style) training as an alternative to explicit Markov-switching models. NLP sentiment features as exogenous variables.

**Key takeaways:**
1. Adversarial training makes forecasters robust to distribution shifts without explicitly modeling regimes — the model learns to perform well under worst-case perturbations
2. Regime collapses (LSTMs failing during high-volatility periods) are addressed by training against synthetic distribution-shifted examples
3. Sentiment from financial text can be incorporated as auxiliary features — Nixtla's `neuralforecast` supports exogenous variables, making this testable

**Technique:** GAN-style adversarial training + LSTM forecaster + NLP sentiment features. Evaluated on US equities.

**Production implication:** The adversarial training angle is interesting but the 9-page paper needs independent replication before trust. The regime-switching problem is real and the explicit approach (Markov-switching in `statsforecast`) is more interpretable. Worth testing both against your current regime handling.
