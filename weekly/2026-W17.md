## [2604.19580] Probabilistic Forecasting for Day-ahead Electricity Prices
**Why relevant:** Direct critique of how probabilistic forecasting models are evaluated in energy/finance — useful for any production TS system where downstream decisions depend on forecast distributions.

**Key takeaways:**
- Battery storage arbitrage (QBTS) is a flawed benchmark for ranking probabilistic forecasts — it ignores intertemporal dependence and creates perverse incentives for dishonest forecasting
- Better approach: frame battery optimization as a stochastic program using full predictive distributions, not quantile summaries
- Better statistical score ≠ better economic decision; evaluation practice needs to connect to actual decision quality

**Technique:** Stochastic programming for decision-quality evaluation; probabilistic (full-distribution) forecasting vs. quantile-based

## [2604.10402] Risk-Sensitive Specialist Routing for Volatility Forecasting
**Why relevant:** Regime-dependent model selection is a practical problem in any production volatility or risk forecasting system; directly applicable to ETF/factor vol forecasting.

**Key takeaways:**
- No single volatility model is consistently best across calm/stressed regimes — model selection should be state-dependent, not static
- Specialist routing (gating between models by regime) cuts high-vol-forecast loss by ~24% vs. rolling-best
- Risk-sensitive online evaluation (not just expected loss) is more appropriate for tail-risk applications

**Technique:** State-dependent gating/specialist routing; risk-sensitive (CVaR-like) online evaluation; rolling walk-forward validation on 6 ETFs

## [2604.09821] Global Persistence, Local Residual Structure: Forecasting Heterogeneous Investment Panels
**Why relevant:** Panel forecasting in finance (credit, equities, macro) always involves mixed data types; this shows how to architect around data-type heterogeneity.

**Key takeaways:**
- Two-stage: global pooled AR for shared persistence + block-specific local models for residuals — beats both pure global and pure local approaches
- Data-type heterogeneity (macro vs. firm-level) is the operative condition, not just cross-sectional heterogeneity
- Tech/health block drives 72% of the gain — high-duration sectors where macro augmentation actively hurts forecasts

**Technique:** Block decomposition by data type; hierarchical reconciliation; panel AR with block-specific residuals; held-out decade validation

## [2604.07159] SBBTS: Schrödinger-Bass Framework for Synthetic Financial Time Series
**Why relevant:** Synthetic data augmentation for financial time series — directly relevant if you have scarce regimes (crises, low-liquidity periods) or cold-start forecasting problems.

**Key takeaways:**
- SBBTS jointly calibrates drift AND stochastic volatility (prior Schrödinger Bridge methods fix one or the other)
- Synthetic S&P 500 series used for data augmentation improve downstream classification accuracy and Sharpe ratio vs. real-data-only training
- Particularly useful for underrepresented regimes and cold-start problems in finance

**Technique:** Schrödinger-Bass Bridge extended to multi-step time series; diffusion-based generative model; conditional transport decomposition
