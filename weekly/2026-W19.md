## [2605.05211] A Review of Large Language Models for Stock Price Forecasting from a Hedge-Fund Perspective

**Why relevant:** Practitioner review (not academic) from a hedge fund — most valuable for its honest accounting of what breaks in production LLM systems, not what works.

**Key takeaways:**
1. The literature's evaluation standards are the real problem — most papers have look-ahead bias via non-causal feature engineering, ignore transaction costs, and use Sharpe ratios without proper risk adjustment
2. The failure modes are well-documented here: sentiment fragility, illiquidity premia, dataset/horizon mismatch, and data leakage in earnings transcript analysis
3. What actually has a fighting chance in production: earnings surprise quantification (bounded noise), slow-moving macro sentiment, options flow integration — not pure price prediction

**Technique:** Survey/review of LLM applications: sentiment extraction, earnings analysis, price tokenization, multi-agent systems.

**Production implication:** If you're benchmarking a new model, follow the paper's rigor checklist: walk-forward out-of-sample, transaction cost modeling, multiple time periods including stress regimes. The failure modes catalogued here apply to any data-driven approach, not just LLMs.

## [2605.06310] Perceive, Route and Modulate: Dynamic Pattern Recalibration for Time Series Forecasting

**Why relevant:** New transformer architecture from Wen/Liang group (Tencent/Kuaishou — consistent TS forecasters). Token-level dynamic recalibration addresses a core problem in financial TS: local patterns drift, but standard transformers apply uniform transformations. Backbone-agnostic adapter mode means it could plug into Nixtla's neuralforecast.

**Key takeaways:**
1. DPR's "Perceive-Route-Modulate" pipeline computes a soft-routing distribution over learned adaptive response patterns — generates per-token modulation applied via residual Hadamard product
2. Backbone-agnostic: improves PatchTST, DLinear, and others with minimal overhead. Standalone DPRNet is competitive with SOTA on 12 benchmarks
3. Validates that macroscopic parameter scaling is not the answer — better mechanisms (dynamic per-token recalibration) beat bigger models

**Technique:** Dynamic Pattern Recalibration — lightweight routing over pattern basis at token level. Residual Hadamard modulation of hidden states.

**Production implication:** Worth testing on financial data specifically — the local pattern drift problem is endemic in finance TS (regime changes, correlation breakdowns). If DPR can be wrapped as an adapter on top of neuralforecast models, it could be a cheap addition to the benchmarking pipeline. Watch for an open-source release.
