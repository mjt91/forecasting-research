## [2605.12099] Bayesian Dynamic Modeling of Realized Volatility in Financial Asset Price Forecasting
**Why relevant:** Mike West (Duke) — Bayesian dynamic models for joint price + realized volatility; directly financial; stat.ME + q-fin.ST dual classification.

**Key takeaways:**
- **Dynamic gamma process** for stochastic volatility + **Bayesian DLMs** for prices/returns — captures vol leverage and feedback effects that GARCH handles awkwardly
- Conjugate-form Bayesian inference: no MCMC, no VI, just fast sequential Kalman-like updates. "Negligible extra computational cost" over standard DLM.
- **Decouple/recouple** extension for multivariate portfolio-level forecasting is the key practical result for production systems
- S&P sector ETF empirical validation with realized volatility from high-frequency intraday data

**Technique:** Dynamic gamma process + Bayesian DLM (Harrison & West), sequential filtering, realized volatility proxies

## [2605.03789] Training-Free Probabilistic Time-Series Forecasting with Conformal Seasonal Pools
**Why relevant:** Manokhin (Oxford/King's) — training-free conformal baseline that 500x beats DeepNPTS on coverage; mandatory calibration standard for production systems.

**Key takeaways:**
- **0.89 empirical 95% coverage** vs DeepNPTS's 0.66 — DeepNPTS systematically fails in worst 10% of windows (all horizons miss simultaneously)
- **500x faster on CPU** — zero learned parameters, no training
- The coverage failure analysis is a masterclass in why aggregate metrics (mean CRPS) hide catastrophic tail failures
- Practical argument: any learned probabilistic forecaster should be tested against CSP as a mandatory baseline before deployment

**Technique:** Conformal prediction + seasonal naive + empirical same-season pooling + signed residual draws

**Contrarian claim:** Learned models may be unnecessary for probabilistic TS forecasting — worth keeping in mind given Nixtla stack.

## [2604.16988] In-Context Learning Under Regime Change
**Why relevant:** Dudley, Bi, Liu, Oymak — regime change detection for transformer ICL; FOMC volatility application without retraining; directly addresses non-stationarity failure mode.

**Key takeaways:**
- **Formal proof** that transformers can learn optimal in-context change-point detection (complexity scales by information available about change-point location)
- **FOMC result**: encoding changepoint knowledge into pretrained models improves financial volatility forecasting around Fed announcements — no retraining needed
- Non-stationarity is the **fundamental problem** in financial TS; standard ICL assumes stationarity and breaks down under regime shifts
- Practical takeaway: when backtesting TS models on financial data, specifically measure performance around known regime-change events

**Technique:** In-context change-point detection, transformer architecture as ICL learner, pretrained foundation model adaptation without fine-tuning
