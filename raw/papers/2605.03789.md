abstract: We propose Conformal Seasonal Pools (CSP), a training-free probabilistic time-series forecaster that mixes same-season empirical draws with signed residual draws around a seasonal naive forecast. In an audited rolling-origin benchmark on six time-series datasets (electricity, exchange_rate, solar_energy, taxi, traffic, wikipedia), CSP-Adaptive significantly outperforms DeepNPTS on every metric reported — CRPS, normalized mean quantile loss, and empirical 95% coverage (0.89 vs 0.66) — while running over 500x faster on CPU. Coverage is decision-critical: a 0.95 nominal interval that contains the truth in only ~66% of cases fails basic calibration and would not survive deployment in safety- or decision-critical settings. CSP achieves all of this with no learned parameters and no training. Training-free conformal samplers should be mandatory baselines when evaluating learned non-parametric forecasters.
